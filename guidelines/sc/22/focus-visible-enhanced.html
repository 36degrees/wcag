Focus Visible (Enhanced)

AA

New

Any keyboard operable user interface has a mode of operation where the keyboard focus indicator meets all of the following:

  • Minimum area: The focus indication area is greater than or equal to a 1 CSS pixel border of the focused control.
  • Change of contrast: Color changes used to indicate focus have at least a 3:1 contrast ratio with the colors changed from the unfocused control.
  • Adjacent contrast: The focus indication area has a 3:1 contrast ratio against all adjacent colors for the minimum area or greater, or has a thickness of at least 2 CSS pixels.

A keyboard focus indicator which has a pattern or gradient may have parts that do not meet the 3:1 contrast ratio for the change of contrast, as long as an area equal to the minimum does meet the contrast ratio.

If the control has a visible boundary smaller than the hit area, the size measure is taken from the visible boundary.
